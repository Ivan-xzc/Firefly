# IELTS writing task 1

The task is to describe what you see. Write a report.Don't give opinions.

**No conclusion.** Write a **summary**.


## 1.line graphs

Your job is to compare the lines, not describe them separately

First, make a very general comparison(**overview paragraph**)

Second compare the lines at specific points(**details' paragraphs**)

Write at least one thing about each line

采用四段式

### Introduction

根据题干修改添加，同义替换

The line graph(The graph below) compares(shows) the amount of electricity produced(electricity production) in France using four different sources of power over a period of 32 years (between 1980 and 2012).

### Overview 

描述两个总体趋势

It is clear that nuclear power was **by far the most important(highest)** means of electricity generation over the period shown. Renewables provided the lowest amount of electricity in each year.

### Details

起始点，转折点之前

In 1980，thermal power stations were the main source of electricity in France, generating around 120 terawatt hour of power. Nuclear and hydroelectric power stations produced just under 75 terawatt hours of electricity each, and renewables provided a **negligible(微忽其微的，可忽略的)** amount. Just one year later, nuclear poweer **overtook** thermal power as the **primary** source of electricity. 

变化趋势

Between 1980 and 2005, electricity production from nuclear power **rose dramatically to a peak** of 430 terawatt hours.**By contrast**, **the figure for** thermal power **fell** to only 50 terawatt hours in 1985, and **remained** at this level for the rest of the period. Hydroeletric power generation **remained relatively stable**, at between 50 and 80 terawatt hours, for the whole 32-year period, but renewable electricity production **saw** only a small rise to **approximately** 25 terawatt hours by 2012.

## 2.bar charts

Bar charts don't always shoe time..., they often only compare numbers

First, make a very general comparison (summary / overview paragraph)

Second, compare detailed numbers (details)

### Introduction 

The bar chart (the chart below) compares the number of mobile phones sold worldwide (global sales) by the five most popular manufacturers (of the top five mobile phones) in the years 2009, 2011, 2013 (between 2009 and 2013).

### Overview - 2 sentences, 2 main points

It is clear that Nokia sold the most mobile phones between 2009 and 2011, but Sambung became the best selling brand in 2013. Samsung and Apple saw the biggest rises in sales over the 5-year period.

### details - group together the items

In 2009, Nokia sold close to 450 million mobile phones, which was **almost double** the number of handsets sold by the **second most successful** manufacturer, Samsung. **Over the following four years**, however, Nokia's **sales figures fell by approximately 200 million units**, **whereas**2 Samsung **saw sales rise by a similar amount**. By 2013, Samsung **had become the market leader** with sales reaching 450 million units.

The other three top selling mobile phone brands between 2009 to 2013 were LG, ZTE and Apple. In 2009, these companies sold around 125 million, 50 million, and 25 million mobile handsets **respectively**, but Apple **overtook** the other two vendors in 2011. In 2013, purchases of Apple handsets reached 150 million units, **while** LG saw declining sales **and** the figures for ZTE rose only **slightly**.
